# BlindAssistant
Capstone Project 626 - A Multimodal Environment Sensing Assistant for Blind and Low Vision Users
Blind and low-vision (BLV) people routinely confront situations where rapid interpretation of the surrounding scene is required, for instance, reading a medication label, locating a door handle, finding misplaced items, or negotiating thresholds such as stairs and narrow doorways. Conventional chat interfaces are ill‑suited to these time‑critical, sensor‑driven tasks. This capstone project proposes a real‑time, on‑device assistant that fuses computer vision (CV) with natural language processing (NLP) to convert camera input and succinct voice intents into short, actionable audio guidance. This multimodal assistant combines camera (for text, object, and affordance detection) and microphone (for brief, natural voice commands) as inputs, and speech (short messages) plus audio (spatialized if possible) as outputs. The assistant operates a tight sense → interpret → act loop on commodity smartphones to provide fast, easily deployable support beyond the chatbot paradigm.
